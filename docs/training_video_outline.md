This sounds like a fantastic initiative. Breaking the training down into a logical progression will help your peers get comfortable with the basics before jumping into the more intense emergency communication features.

Here is a proposed 5-part YouTube video series outline, structured to take operators from casual check-ins to managing multi-monitor EOC setups.

### Video 1: Getting Started with ECT Logger (The Participant Experience)

**Target Audience:** All net participants, casual check-ins, and local club members.
**Goal:** Show how frictionless it is to get into the system and participate in a net.

* **Introduction:** Brief overview of what ECT Logger is and why it beats clunky desktop apps.
* **Frictionless Sign-In:** Demonstrating the magic link email authentication and OAuth (Google, Microsoft, GitHub) so users know they don't have to manage another password.
* **Navigating the Dashboard:** Toggling between Card and List views to find active and scheduled nets.
* **The Participant View:** How to view the live check-in list and read the station status indicators (Checked In, Just Listening, Available, Away, Checked Out).
* **Engagement:** Participating in the chat, answering the "Topic of the Week," and voting in live Polls.
* **Location Awareness:** Showing how to enable the auto-fill feature for Maidenhead grid squares based on browser location.

### Video 2: Stepping Up to Net Control (Running Your First Net)

**Target Audience:** Operators taking on the NCS role for weekly or casual nets.
**Goal:** Prove that running a net is simple and intuitive.

* **Creating the Net:** Setting up the name, description, and initial frequencies.
* **The Net Script:** How to load and read from the formatted net scripts to keep operations running smoothly.
* **Logging Check-ins (Two Ways):** * *Method 1:* Clicking a row for inline editing to update individual station details.
* *Method 2:* Smashing the **Speed Entry (⏩)** button to quickly log a string of callsigns during a fast-moving pileup.


* **Mapping the Net:** Viewing check-ins on the OpenStreetMap and explaining the supported location formats (Town/State, GPS, Maidenhead, UTM, MGRS).
* **Wrapping Up:** Closing the net and showing the automated email logs that get sent out.

### Video 3: Advanced Operations: Multi-Op & Multi-Frequency Nets

**Target Audience:** ARES, SKYWARN, and emergency communicators.
**Goal:** Showcase ECT Logger's ability to handle complex, multi-agency incidents.

* **Role Delegation:** How the primary NCS can assign "Secondary NCS," "Logger," and "Relay" roles to distribute the workload.
* **Managing Multiple Frequencies:** Adding and tracking multiple bands, channels, or modes within a single active net.
* **Tracking Stations:** How to log stations checking in (and rechecking) across different frequencies.
* **The Active Frequency Indicator:** Showing how participants can see exactly which frequency each NCS is currently monitoring in real-time.
* **Custom Fields (Admin Highlight):** Setting up specific data points like SKYWARN spotter numbers or weather observations.

### Video 4: After-Action: Reports, Analytics & ICS-309

**Target Audience:** Net Managers, Emergency Coordinators, and Record Keepers.
**Goal:** Highlight how the platform automates the tedious paperwork following an operation.

* **Emergency Documentation:** Generating and downloading the official ICS-309 Communications Log with a single click.
* **Comprehensive PDF Exports:** Pulling the full multi-page PDF report for a closed net (including check-ins, chat logs, and statistics).
* **Schedule Statistics & Leaderboards:** Exploring the analytics dashboard. Filtering by time windows (30d / 90d / 1y / all-time) to view participation trends and NCS/Logger leaderboards.
* **Automated Scheduling:** Setting up a recurring schedule and managing the automated NCS rotation and reminder emails.

### Video 5: Power User Tips & Workspace Customization

**Target Audience:** Frequent operators, SysAdmins, and EOC station managers.
**Goal:** Show off the quality-of-life "bonus" features that make long sessions comfortable.

* **Detaching Windows for Multi-Monitor Setups:** A walkthrough of unsnapping and popping out the check-in list and chat into separate browser windows—perfect for spreading out across EOC displays.
* **Dark Mode:** Toggling the dark theme to save eye strain during long operational periods or late-night weather events.
* **Advanced Filtering & Sorting:** Using the search tools to quickly find historical nets, specific schedules, frequencies, or users.
* **Sign-off:** A quick wrap-up, thanking operators for their time, and providing links to the GitHub repository and documentation.

**Production Tip:** Keep the videos relatively short (3–6 minutes each). You can always bundle them into a single YouTube Playlist called "ECT Logger Mastery" so operators can binge them or just pick the specific topic they need help with.
